12 · 1-2-2 Zone Defense

Wing Entry · Corner Occupied

6 Phases

Phase 1 — Base Alignment

1
Set your starting point before the offense sets theirs. Every defender finds their spot — 1 at the top, 2 and 3 at the elbows, 4 and 5 at the blocks — before the offense moves. The zone lives or dies by its starting shape.
2
Know the offensive set you're defending. The offense is 4-out: a point guard at the top (X1), two wings (X2/X3), a high post (X4), and one corner (X5). Before the ball moves, identify who is in your area. 2 and 3 share responsibility for X4 at the high post — both elbow defenders deny that interior entry together.
3
No drifting. The most common error is defenders sagging too deep or creeping too high before the ball moves. Stay in your starting position. React to the ball, don't guess where it's going. Trust your starting point. Trust your teammates.
Coach's Eye — Alignment The 1-2-2 needs to look tight from the offense's perspective. A well-set zone is one where every gap looks covered before the ball moves. If the top and elbow defenders are too spread, the offense attacks the gaps. If the blocks are too deep, they can't react up.
Structural Bridge → Phase 2 The moment X1 passes to a wing, the base alignment becomes Phase 2. The key question: is there a corner player on the ball side? In this system, there is — which means Phase 2 is the corner-occupied response. Every defender reads the pass and shifts simultaneously.

Phase 2 — Wing Entry · Corner Occupied

1
2 closes out on the ball-side wing (X2). When the ball enters the right wing, 2 sprints to close out — hand up, no foul, feet active. You are now on-ball. Force them baseline or back to the top. Contest every catch, every shot.
2
1 drops to the ball-side elbow. As 2 closes out, 1 follows — filling the vacated right elbow zone. This is a relay: 2 takes the ball, 1 fills 2's spot. Deny the elbow pass and stay between the ball and X4.
3
4 shifts to the short corner — not the block. X5 is in the corner. 4 positions between the wing (ball) and X5. Corner occupied = you leave the block. Deny the easy corner feed.
4
5 slides right to just outside the key to front the high-post area. Deny any interior entry. Stay between X4 and the ball. Move your feet, stay active.
5
3 drops straight down the left lane line toward the weak-side block. Face the ball. Protect the paint on the weak side. React to any skip pass to X3 or cutter through the lane.
Coach's Eye — The Chain This works because it's a chain reaction, not individual choices. Ball to wing → 2 closes out → 1 drops elbow → 4 reads corner → 5 fronts post → 3 drops baseline. Everyone talks. Everyone communicates responsibilities. If one link is slow, the whole coverage breaks.
Structural Bridge → Phase 3 With all five defenders in position, Phase 3 breaks down each defender's read — what to do if the ball moves, if a cutter comes through, or if the offense tries to skip. The zone is set. Now it has to react.

Phase 3 — Reads & Responsibilities

1
2 reads: If the ball drives baseline, contest and funnel to help. If they pass to the corner, jam the passing lane — you're between ball and X5. You guard the ball. Make them decide.
2
1 reads: If the ball comes back to the top, 1 recovers to the top of the zone. If the offense attacks the elbow gap, 1 contests. The elbow is the weakest spot in most zones — protect it with urgency.
3
4 reads: If the pass goes to the corner (X5), 4 contests the catch. Ball is now at X5 — this triggers Phase 4 reads. If ball reverses, 4 slides back toward the block.
4
5 reads: Front the low post. 5's only responsibility is denying the ball to any low-post threat. No ball in the block. Stay between the ball and the basket. Physical, demanding, non-negotiable.
5
3 reads: If there is a skip pass to X3 (weak-side wing), 3 must close out hard. If a cutter drives through the lane, 3 is there to stop them. Guard your area — your job changes when the ball moves.
Coach's Eye — Ball Watching The most common breakdown in zone defense is defenders watching the ball and forgetting their area. When you see the ball, also see your area. Zone defense isn't standing still — it's five players moving together every time the ball moves.
Structural Bridge — Reset If the ball is passed to X5 in the corner, Phase 4 activates. If the ball reverses back, Phase 4 shows the recovery. Every possession is a series of micro-phases. Trust the shape. Read the ball. Communicate every pass.

Phase 4 — X5 Passes to X2 (Reversal)

1
2 must read the pass and recover fast. When X5 returns the ball to X2, the strong-side coverage has to reset. 2 sprints back toward X2 — read the pass in the air and close out before X2 can shoot. The corner-to-wing pass is slower than a skip pass, but you still have to move.
2
1 holds the elbow. The ball is back at the right wing — 1 holds the ball-side elbow position from Phase 2. No need to chase the ball all the way back to the top. Stay at the elbow, read X4, and communicate with 2.
3
4 and 5 hold their Phase 2 shape. With ball back at the right wing, 4 is in the short corner and 5 fronts the post. The zone never fully collapsed — it just flexed and is snapping back.
Coach's Eye — Recovery Speed This phase tests whether your zone is disciplined or lazy. If 2 is still sagging from Phase 3 when X2 catches, the offense gets an open look. Read the pass early. Move before it arrives.
Structural Bridge → Phase 5 If X2 passes back to X1 at the top, the zone fully resets to base alignment — Phase 5. The offense has completed a swing around the perimeter without a scoring opportunity. That's the zone doing its job.

Phase 5 — X2 Passes to X1 (Full Reset)

1
1 sprints back to the top of the zone. Ball at the top means 1 must be there. This recovery is non-negotiable — if 1 is still at the elbow when X1 catches, the offense attacks the top gap. Run, don't jog.
2
2 drops back to the right elbow. No wing threat means 2 returns to base. This is a reset — all five defenders returning to Phase 1 positions. The zone is breathing, ready to react to the next pass.
3
3 holds left elbow — already home. 3 hasn't had to chase the ball this entire possession. But 3 must stay alert — X1 at the top might attack left next. X3 is still on the weak side wing.
Coach's Eye — The Zone is Alive A full reset is a win for the defense. The offense moved the ball around the perimeter and couldn't crack the zone. Now it starts again. Every time the zone resets cleanly, it wears on the offense mentally.
Structural Bridge → Phase 6 If X1 swings the ball to the LEFT wing (X3), the zone mirrors Phase 2 on the opposite side. Every concept is the same — 3 closes out, 1 drops to left elbow, 4 short corner left, 5 fronts post. Same reads, other side.

Phase 6 — X1 Passes to X3 (Mirrored)

1
3 closes out on X3 — mirror of 2's Phase 2 role. The ball went left. 3 is the closer on this side. Sprint to X3, hand up, force them baseline or back to the top. Every principle from Phase 2 applies — same defense, other side of the court.
2
1 drops to the LEFT elbow — mirror of Phase 2. As 3 closes out, 1 drops to the left elbow, filling the zone 3 vacated. Same relay — 3 takes ball, 1 fills elbow. Deny any interior entry to X4.
3
4 shifts to SHORT CORNER LEFT. X5 is now in the left corner — occupied. 4 slides to the left short corner, positioned between X3 (ball) and X5 (corner). Same read as Phase 2, mirrored to the left side.
4
5 slides left to front the post. 5 moves across the key from the right side to front X4. Same post fronting responsibility — just from the opposite starting point. Deny the interior entry. No ball to the post.
5
2 drops right lane line toward weak-side block. 2 mirrors what 3 did in Phase 2 — drop from the right elbow straight down the lane line. Protect the paint on the weak side. Face the ball, watch for skip to X2.
Coach's Eye — The Zone is Symmetrical The 1-2-2 works the same on both sides. There is no weak side in this zone — only ball-side and help-side. If your players know their reads on the right, they know them on the left. Repetition makes this automatic.
